How it works

Monitoring-driven DNS automation.

Unlike competitors who keep DNS and monitoring separate, Total Uptime integrates them. When a server fails, DNS updates automatically.

  1. 1

    Configure pools

    Group servers into failover pools with primary and secondary targets and assign monitors.

  2. 2

    Continuous checks

    Global vantage points monitor via Ping, HTTP, HTTPS, DNS, or custom app checks.

  3. 3

    Validate failure

    Recovery control

FAQ

Quick answers to the questions infrastructure teams ask most.

Need more? Our team is on the phone 24/7/365 — including setup.

What's the difference between authoritative and comprehensive DNS?

+

Authoritative DNS just hosts records and answers queries — no monitoring, no failover. When a server dies, DNS keeps pointing at the dead box until someone updates it manually. Comprehensive DNS hosts the records and continuously monitors your servers; when a failure is detected, DNS auto-updates to healthy infrastructure.

How does DNS failover actually work?

+

Health monitors detect unavailability via Ping, HTTP, HTTPS or a custom check. The DNS layer automatically removes the failed server from responses and directs traffic to healthy members of the pool. Configurable thresholds, manual overrides and maintenance windows let you stay in control.

Can I run Total Uptime as a secondary DNS provider?

+

Yes. Full AXFR and IXFR zone transfers with TSIG authentication — run us as primary or secondary alongside an existing provider for true multi-provider redundancy.
